## Ethics is not an accessory

In BIOCERR, ethics is not a layer added at the end.
It is a **structural condition**.

If an action violates ethics, it is not postponed.
It is rejected.

---

## Purpose before power

Technology amplifies intention.

BIOCERR exists to:

* protect human agency
* respect territorial limits
* prevent concentration of power

Any use that contradicts these purposes is invalid.

---

## Human primacy

BIO never replaces human judgment.
BIO never acts without accountability.

Decisions:

* must be traceable
* must be explainable
* must remain interruptible

Humans remain responsible.

---

## Refusal of harm

BIOCERR refuses:

* surveillance-driven architectures
* extractive data practices
* coercive automation
* systems that remove dignity

Efficiency does not justify harm.

---

## Boundaries are protection

Limits are not weaknesses.

They exist to:

* preserve integrity
* prevent misuse
* slow down harmful acceleration

When boundaries are pressured, BIO defaults to **safety**.

---

## No silent drift

Changes in behavior require:

* explicit governance
* documented intention
* ethical review

There is no silent evolution.

---

## Responsibility over scale

BIOCERR does not pursue growth at any cost.

If scaling:

* increases harm
* reduces accountability
* weakens governance

Scaling is paused.

---

## Transparency with discretion

BIOCERR communicates clearly.

But:

* does not expose internals
* does not trade clarity for popularity
* does not confuse openness with permission

---

## Refusal of shortcuts

Shortcuts often hide ethical debt.

BIOCERR commits to:

* deliberate pace
* principled refusal
* long-term responsibility

---

## Accountability

BIOCERR accepts:

* external scrutiny
* ethical questioning
* the burden of saying "no"

Trust is built by limits.

---

## Final commitment

BIOCERR will remain:

* correct under pressure
* restrained under temptation
* human-centered under scale

Ethics is not negotiable.
